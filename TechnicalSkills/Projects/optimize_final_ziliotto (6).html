Cleaning and Standardizing Game Information¶

When working with game data, I needed to clean the game info column to make it more uniform and easier to use. Specifically, I replaced '@' with 'vs' and removed any additional information, such as date and time, that appeared after a space. This process was applied to multiple DataFrames.

Steps Taken:¶

Replace '@' with 'vs': Since '@' often signifies an away game, I standardized it by converting it to 'vs', making the notation consistent.

Remove Unnecessary Information: Using .str.split(" ").str[0], I extracted only the first part of the game info string, eliminating date/time details.

Apply to Multiple DataFrames: These transformations were applied to merged_def, merged_off, and combined_df2 to ensure consistency across datasets.

Calculating Offensive Total Fantasy Points¶

When designing a fantasy football scoring system, I needed to calculate total fantasy points based on various player stats. I structured the formula to account for passing, rushing, receiving, and miscellaneous scoring factors.

Steps Taken:¶

  1. Passing Stats:

    • Award 4 points per passing touchdown (pass td).
    • Grant 0.04 points per passing yard (equivalent to 1 point per 25 yards).
    • Add a 3-point bonus for 300+ passing yards.
    • Deduct 1 point per interception (pass int).

  2. Rushing Stats:

    • Award 6 points per rushing touchdown (rush td).
    • Grant 0.1 points per rushing yard (equivalent to 1 point per 10 yards).
    • Add a 3-point bonus for 100+ rushing yards.

  3. Receiving Stats:

    • Award 6 points per receiving touchdown (rcv td).
    • Grant 0.1 points per receiving yard (equivalent to 1 point per 10 yards).
    • Add a 3-point bonus for 100+ receiving yards.
    • Grant 1 point per reception (rec).

  4. Miscellaneous Stats:

    • Award 6 points per punt, kickoff, or field goal return touchdown.
    • Deduct 1 point per lost fumble (fl).
    • Award 2 points per successful two-point conversion (2pt conversions).
    • Award 6 points per offensive fumble recovery touchdown.

    • Award 1 point per sack (sack).
    • Award 2 points per interception (interception).
    • Award 2 points per fumble recovery (fumble recovery).
    • Award 6 points for touchdowns scored off interceptions (interception return td).
    • Award 6 points for touchdowns scored off fumble recoveries (fumble recovery td).
    • Award 6 points for touchdowns from blocked punts or field goals (blocked punt or fg return td).
    • Award 2 points per safety (safety).
    • Award 2 points per blocked kick (blocked kicks).
    • Award 2 points for defensive returns of a two-point conversion (2pt conversions).

    • Grant 10 points if the defense allows 0 points.
    • Grant 7 points if the defense allows 1-6 points.
    • Grant 4 points if the defense allows 7-13 points.
    • Grant 1 point if the defense allows 14-20 points.
    • Deduct 1 point if the defense allows 28-34 points.
    • Deduct 4 points if the defense allows 35 or more points.

# defining model
model = pe.ConcreteModel()

# binary decision variables
model.y = pe.Var(DV_indexes, domain=pe.Binary)

# objective function (maximize total fantasy points)
model.obj = pe.Objective(
    expr=sum(combined_df.loc[i, 'total fantasy points'] * model.y[i] for i in DV_indexes),
    sense=pe.maximize
)

# constraints
model.tot_players = pe.Constraint(expr=sum(model.y[i] for i in DV_indexes) == 9) # nine total players on team
model.tot_salary = pe.Constraint(expr=sum(combined_df.loc[i, 'salary'] * model.y[i] for i in DV_indexes) <= 50000) # salaries sum to <= $50,000

Explanation:¶

This model was similar to the one I used in the naive strategy above, but the objective function became maximizing boosted avg points per game. This column was created during the cleaning and reconfiguration of data phase before problem 1. I compiled factors I believed would indicate better results from players for week 17 and assigned multiplier values to weight players more heavily if they fit the criteria. These multipliers were then multiplied by the avg points per game and put into the new column boosted avg points per game.

The criteria I chose started with whether they had started 16 or more games in the season and then whether they were set to play a home game or not. I figured that players with 16 or more starting games were fairly reliable and well regarded by their coaches; therefore, more likely to consistently do well. Additionally, players playing the home game have the advantage of fan support and familiarity, so I felt they were likely to do better.

For the position-specific criteria, I looked at indicators of quality players or factors that would give a position an advantage. For players that were DST and playing a weak defense team (figured this out by graphing the defense teams by their avg points per game), I said give those players the extra multiplier because they are likely to do better. For QB, if they had pass yds >= 3750, they got the multiplier because 4000 yds is considered to be a top player, which was a little too restrictive. I felt 3750 yds had a little more room for an optimal QB choice. For WR, if they had rcv yds >= 1000 yds, I gave them the multiplier because that is considered to be a great amount for a full season and indicates the player is playing well overall. For TE with rcv yds >= 800, I gave them the multiplier because that is considered to be a great score indicative of a player consistently performing well. For both WR and TE players with rcv td >= 10, I gave them the multiplier because it was in the middle of 12, which is considered to be the best, and 9, which is considered good. Touchdowns and receiving yards are good indicators of a WR and TE's performance overall.

The constraints are identical to the naive model, apart from adding age to the mix, with the constraint age <= 30. I added this because it is unlikely a player over the age of 30 is going to be playing that late in the season without some kind of fatigue or injury. Football retirement is an average age of 27.6, which is still below the 30 threshold I set.

Looking at the results of the model, we see a boosted avg points per game total of 174.25 and the salary total being $50,000.00.
